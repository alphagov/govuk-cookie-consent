Cookies on GOV.UK

Cookies are files saved on your phone, tablet or computer when you visit a website.

We use cookies to collect and store information about how you use the GOV.UK website and government digital services, such as the pages you visit. 'Government digital services' means any page with service.gov.uk in the URL.

This page has a brief explanation of each type of cookie we use. Read our detailed cookie information if you want to know more.

Cookie settings

We use 4 types of cookies. You can choose which cookies you're happy for us to use.

Cookies that measure website use

We use Google Analytics cookies to measure how you use GOV.UK and government digital services. These cookies collect information about things like:

  • how you got to these sites
  • the pages you visit
  • how long you spend on each page on GOV.UK
  • what you click on while you're visiting these sites

We collect this information to improve the website.

We use Google Analytics cookies to collect information in two ways:

  • measure general website usage
  • understand user behaviour in more detail

Youâ€™re automatically opted in for gathering general information about how the website is used. You can change your cookie preferences below.

We also use LUX Real User Monitoring software cookies from SpeedCurve to for example measure how well the website performed on your device during your visit and if there were any JavaScript errors.

We do not allow Google or SpeedCurve to use or share this data for their own purposes.
